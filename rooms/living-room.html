Pro Tips

3 Living Room Rules for Lasting Order

Every item needs a home before it enters the room

The living room collects clutter because it's where people drop things without a plan. Before anything stays in the room permanently, decide where it lives — a basket, a shelf, a drawer. No designated home means it goes elsewhere.

Use decorative storage as your first layer

Beautiful baskets and ottomans with storage aren't just for aesthetics — they're your first line of defense against visual clutter. Anything that needs to be in the living room but isn't beautiful goes inside them.

Do a nightly reset — 5 items back to their homes

Instead of a full tidy session, commit to returning just five items to their homes before bed each night. This microhabit prevents the Saturday afternoon overhaul and keeps the room permanently manageable.
