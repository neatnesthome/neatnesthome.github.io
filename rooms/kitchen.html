Pro Tips

3 Kitchen Organization Rules That Actually Work

Zone by task, not by category

Keep everything you need for morning coffee in one zone, baking supplies in another, and snacks in a dedicated spot. When items live where you use them, putting things back becomes automatic.

Decant before you organize

Original packaging is the enemy of an organized pantry. Move dry goods into clear airtight containers first — it eliminates half-open bags and lets you see at a glance what needs restocking.

Label everything, including fill dates

A labeled canister tells you what's inside. A labeled canister with a "filled on" date tells you when to toss it. Add dates when you decant and you'll never eat stale flour again.
